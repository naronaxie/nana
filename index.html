My motivation for learning to code

Posted on July 6, 2020

This blog post is intended to explain why I have decided to learn HTML/CSS and JavaScript. I began taking the Khan Academy courses before I began university, but became too busy to keep up with it. During college, I studied a diverse range of technologies and computer science was one of them. I learned a variety of languages and IEDs during my undergraduate career, but never had any practical application for it. Because of this, my skills went unused and I eventually lost parts of my knowledge. After graduating, I decided to change my career path and focus on UI/UX design. However, because of the COVID-19 pandemic, the limited job opportunities has caused me to expand my knowledge and skills of coding more. Hopefully by the end of this course I can apply my artistic skills with my coding knowledge to create fully functional web pages for clients.

What I have learned so far...

Posted on July 6, 2020

As stated in my previous entry, I did study a couple semesters of computer science. I learned Khan Academy's version of JavaScript, Processing's version of Java, and C# for Unity. After getting a basis of how to use HTML/CSS, I plan to learn JQuery so I can make interactive websites. So far I have learned a great deal about logic and how to think about code. I also took a semester learning about computer graphics and learned more math concepts involving matrix multiplication, algorithms to draw basic shapes from scratch, and more. I also learned how to make video games in Unity using C#. Overall I would say that I have a good understanding of syntax and thinking at a basic level, but haven't had the experience to start a project from scratch and connect it to back end engines. Luckily I have great resources around me, so I will be able to accomplish it in time.
